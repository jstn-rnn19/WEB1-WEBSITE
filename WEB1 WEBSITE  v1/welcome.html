50/30/20 Rules

The 50/30/20 Rule is a popular and straightforward budgeting guideline designed to help individuals manage their money effectively and consistently save for the future.

50% for Needs

This category is for the non-negotiable expenses you absolutely must pay to live and maintain your job. If you lost your income, these are the payments you would struggle to defer or eliminate.

Goal: Spend no more than 30% of your net income on items you desire.

Examples: Rent or Mortgage payments, Groceries, Minimum Debt Payments, Utilities (water, electricity), Health Insurance, Essential Transportation (gas, public transit pass).

30% for Wants

This category is focused entirely on your future financial health and should be treated as non-negotiable, just like your Needs.

Goal: Spend no more than 30% of your net income on items you desire.

Examples: Dining out, Entertainment subscriptions (Netflix, Spotify), Hobbies, Travel and Vacations, Non-essential Clothing purchases, Premium coffee, Gym Memberships.

20% for Savings & Debt

This category is focused entirely on your future financial health and should be treated as non-negotiable, just like your Needs.

Goal: Commit a minimum of 20% of your net income to these goals.

Examples: Contributions to an emergency fund, retirement accounts (401k, IRA), or long-term savings for a large purchase (house, car).
